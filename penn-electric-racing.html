Lead Project

Supercapacitor Charging & Power Management

Project Overview

As Subsystem Hardware Engineer Lead, I led an 8-member team on the electrical design of the car's Dashboard and Data Acquisition (DAQ) power system. The work centered on the team's first supercapacitor-based backup circuit, which holds up the MCU rail and guarantees a safe, controlled shutdown on a loss of primary power. I designed the boards in Altium and simulated the primary supply in LTspice.

Power Control Module (PCM) schematic: the supercap sense and charge paths feeding the DAQ and Dashboard power system.

Results

  • Integrated the team's first supercapacitor-based backup circuit for safe MCU shutdown on power loss
  • Improved power-system efficiency by 15% (up to 95%)
  • Reduced board space by 20%
  • Primary supply designed in Altium and simulated in LTspice

Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Testing System

Project Overview

Developed a sophisticated testing system that enables Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) testing to validate critical vehicle functions. The system specifically ensures proper operation of crucial components like the brake sensor, Ready-To-Drive-Sound (RTDS), and Super Capacitor (SCAP) systems. This testing adapter is instrumental in validating critical functions that interface between both the Power Control Module (PCM) and the Data Acquisition Dashboard (DAQDASH).

  • Automated test sequence capabilities

  • Configurable fault injection for robustness testing
