Lead Project

Supercapacitor Charging & Power Management

Project Overview

As Subsystem Hardware Engineer Lead, I led an 8-member team on the electrical design of the car's Dashboard and Data Acquisition (DAQ) power system. The work centered on the team's first supercapacitor-based backup circuit, which holds up the MCU rail and guarantees a safe, controlled shutdown on a loss of primary power. I designed the boards in Altium and simulated the primary supply in LTspice.

The supercapacitor charging circuit: charge-controller IC, synchronous buck output, input switch, and SCAP control logic.

Power management module: ideal-diode input protection and common-mode filtering, 5V and 3.3V synchronous bucks, the supercapacitor step-down backup, and a 5V external load switch.

Altium layout of the power test board used to bring up and validate the supply.

Results

  • Integrated the team's first supercapacitor-based backup circuit for safe MCU shutdown on power loss
  • Improved power-system efficiency by 15% (up to 95%)
  • Reduced board space by 20%
  • Primary supply designed in Altium and simulated in LTspice

Dashboard & DAQ Board (DAQDASH)

Project Overview

The Dashboard and Data Acquisition board (DAQDASH) is the physical board that carries the power system I led. It brings together the supercapacitor backup, the 5V and 3.3V regulators, ideal-diode input protection, load switching, and the dashboard's data-acquisition and indicator circuitry on a single custom multi-layer PCB, laid out in Altium.

3D render of the REV11 DAQDASH v2 board.

Altium board layout.

The assembled board, populated and ready for bring-up.

Reverse side of the assembled board.

Technical Details

  • Multi-layer board designed in Altium Designer
  • Integrated 5V and 3.3V synchronous buck regulators with ideal-diode input protection
  • Supercapacitor backup with controlled load switching for safe shutdown
  • On-board data acquisition and status indicators for the driver dashboard

Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Testing System

Project Overview

Developed a sophisticated testing system that enables Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) testing to validate critical vehicle functions. The system specifically ensures proper operation of crucial components like the brake sensor, Ready-To-Drive-Sound (RTDS), and Super Capacitor (SCAP) systems. This testing adapter is instrumental in validating critical functions that interface between both the Power Control Module (PCM) and the Data Acquisition Dashboard (DAQDASH).
